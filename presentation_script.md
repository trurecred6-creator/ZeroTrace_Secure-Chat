# ZeroTrace Presentation Script

Here are two versions of a script you can use to explain your project. The first is in "Layman's English" (everyday language for a general audience), and the second is in "Easy English" (very simple, step-by-step language).

Both scripts follow the structure of your problem statement: introducing the challenge, highlighting the required features, and then revealing your novel solution.

---

## Option 1: Layman's English (General Audience)

**[Introduction & Problem Statement]**
"Hello everyone. Today I want to show you **ZeroTrace**. 
Our initial challenge was a clear but difficult problem: We needed to build a secure mobile chat prototype. In a world where data leaks are strictly common, we had to ensure that when two people talk, absolutely no one else can listen in."

**[Showing the Features]**
"To solve this, our project had to support three critical features:
First, **End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)**. This means messages are locked before they leave your phone and can only be unlocked by the person receiving them. 
Second, **Self-Destructing Messages**. For highly sensitive information, users need a timer that automatically wipes the message from both devices once time runs out.
And third, **Basic Anonymity**. There should be no central server logging who you are or who you are talking to."

**[The Novel Solution]**
"Now, lots of apps *claim* to have these features, but they act like a 'black box'—you just have to trust them. 
Our novel solution with ZeroTrace is that we don't just ask for your trust; we **prove it**. 
We built an interactive, dual-view simulator. On one screen, you can see both the Sender's phone and the Receiver's phone side-by-side. When you send a message, ZeroTrace instantly opens up the 'black box' and shows you the exact cryptographic keys, the SHA-256 hashes, and the mathematical decryption process firing in real-time between the devices. 
It’s not just a secure chat app; it is a live educational tool that makes invisible security completely transparent."

## Option 2: Easy English (Very Simple & Direct)

**[Introduction & Problem Statement]**
"Hi everyone. This is my project, **ZeroTrace**. 
The goal of this project was to make a very safe chat app for phones. Today, it is too easy for hackers or companies to steal our private messages. We wanted to stop that."

**[Showing the Features]**
"To make the app safe, we added three main features:
1. **End-to-End Encryption:** This is like putting a strong lock on your text message. Only your friend has the key to open it.
2. **Auto-Delete Timers:** If you send a secret, you can set a timer. After a few seconds, the message deletes itself forever.
3. **Hidden Identity:** We don't save your real name or phone number on any central server. You stay completely anonymous."

**[The Novel Solution]**
"The biggest problem with other safe apps is that you can't *see* the security working. You just have to guess that it is safe.
Our special, novel solution is how we built the app. We made a split-screen simulator. You can see the Sender's phone on the left, and the Receiver's phone on the right. 
When you type a message, our app pauses and actually shows you the secret math and the digital locks being created in the middle. We let you click an 'Info' button on every single message to see its unique security log. 
We didn't just build a safe app; we built a window so you can watch the safety happen with your own eyes."
